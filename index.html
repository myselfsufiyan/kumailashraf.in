Shaykh Syed Kumail Ashraf al'Ashrafi ul'Jilani — "Shaykh'ul'Hind"

Scholar • Sufi guide • Orator • Founder of educational & religious institutions
Ashrafiya Qadri & Chishti Orator

Biography (short)

Born in 1932–33 into the historic Ashrafi household, Shaykh Syed Kumail Ashraf was educated at Daar ul Uloom Ashrafiya, Mubarakpur, graduating in 1957. For around six decades he served as an orator, teacher and spiritual guide, carrying forward a lineage that traces back to Abdul Qadir Jilani and Makhdoom Syed Ashraf Jahangir Simnani.

After receiving khilafat from his father and other senior peers, he led and expanded the Ashrafi sufi orders, travelled widely (including extensive work in Mumbai, Goa and the UAE), and built madrasas, mosques and khanqahs which provide free education and lodging to students and travellers.

Contributions & Institutions

  • Founder — Khanqah-e‑Ashrafiya (Kichaucha): large complex with mosque, guest rooms and congregation hall.
  • Established Daar ul Uloom Mehboob e Yazdani (Baskhari, 1975) and Aziza Muslim Girls School (1985) — both free institutions.
  • Founded madrasas in Vasco (Goa) and Daar ul Uloom Makhdoom e Simnani (Mumbra, 2008).
  • Active preaching and mosque work in Mumbai (Al Madina Mosque, Agripada) and UAE (mosques in Dubai).

Spiritual life & Teaching

He was a devoted mureed of his father and received khilafat in the Aaliya Qadriya Ashrafiya and Aaliya Chishti Ashrafiya orders. Known for spontaneous and moving sermons, he was invited to national conferences from a young age and held great influence across religious circles.

Poetry & Oratory

Alongside preaching he wrote poetry under the pen name Kumail. His speeches—especially on the martyrdom of Imam Husain—are remembered for stirring deep emotion and devotion in audiences.

"A rare kind of spontaneity in speech — the saying of a wali is like the saying of God."

Selected Writings & Speeches

Various articles and speeches published across magazines and books. His oratory drew comparisons to classical poets and mystic writers, and he frequently participated in major conferences in India and abroad.
